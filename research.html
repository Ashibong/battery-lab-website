Simulation & Prediction (ÏãúÎÆ¨Î†àÏù¥ÏÖò Î∞è ÏòàÏ∏°)

Our aim is to deeply investigate and predict the phenomenon of thermal runaway (TR)‚Äîa core safety issue in lithium-ion batteries‚Äîand its propagation mechanisms. A high-precision simulation model will be developed, enabling the observation of the entire process from initiation to propagation of thermal runaway in a virtual environment. Through this approach, the physical and chemical characteristics of each stage will be quantitatively analyzed. Ultimately, this research aims to provide key predictive technologies for establishing battery system design and safety standards, thereby making a significant contribution to the enhancement of battery safety.

  • Thermal Runaway and Propagation Mechanism
  • Simulate Alltime Process of Thermal Runaway

We develop physics-based multi-scale and multi-physics models, and AI-based models, spanning from materials to packs and systems. These models support degradation and lifetime prediction, electrochemical, mechanical, and thermal analyses, etc. Our approach enables accelerated understanding of complex battery behaviors and systematic exploration of diverse operating scenarios, while providing predictive insights across scales.

  • Multi-scale, multi-physics modeling
  • Degradation prediction and lifetime estimation
  • System-level simulations under real-world conditions

Design & Optimization (ÏÑ§Í≥Ñ Î∞è ÏµúÏ†ÅÌôî)

We build design and optimization frameworks for cells, modules, packs, and systems using data from experiments and simulations. These frameworks balance performance, safety, and cost under engineering constraints through physics-based methods. Our approach identifies how characteristics and behavior may change from cells to systems, guiding designs that are valid and applicable across cell, module, pack, and system levels.

  • Battery cell, module, and pack design frameworks
  • Physics-based multi-scale, multi-physics optimization
  • Balancing performance, safety, and cost trade-offs
